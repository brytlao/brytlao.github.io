Engineering

I've been involved in multiple projects requiring me to prototype and develop systems.
Most of these systems require logging of sensor information and interaction with users.
The relevant tools are included in the descriptions.

Motion Capture

Motion Capture (Mocap) is the process of recording movement. I have spent thousands of hours capturing and post-processing human motion for research. Occasionally, objects are also included in the capture.
Tools: Mac3D, nMotion, Matlab

  • See more

KinectV2

The Kinect is a motion sensing input device developed and sold by Microsoft. It serves as a nice subsitute for the Mocap system in terms of portability and price. I interface it with other sensor systems, such as Myo and Mac3D.
Tools: KinectV2, Processing, VisualC#

  • See more

Myo Armband

The Myo is an armband with EMG and IMU sensors, developed and sold by Thalmic Labs (now North). Although it doesn't reach clinical standards for EMG quality, interesting applications are possible. I interface it with other sensor systems, such as Kinect and Lego Mindstorms.
Tools: Myo Connect, Processing

  • See more

Leap Motion

The Leap Motion is a hand and finger motion sensor, developed and sold by Leap Motion, Inc. I used the device as a 2-dimensional controller to modulate various touch properties, for a linear vibrator. The rest of the system was used to detect human tactile sweep thresholds.
Tools: Leap Motion, VisualC#

  • See more

Pressure Glove

The Pressure Glove was developed mainly using the ShokacChip, a multi-axis tactile sensor. I was in charge of developing a finger-pressure logging system that communicates with other devices, such as Mac3D.
Tools: ShokacChip, Processing, CAD, Opcode

  • See more

Shepherding Simulation

The shepherding task describes the dynamics between a dog and a flock of sheep. The dog needs to herd the flock to a predefined location, while collecting sheep that stray too far from the flock. This simulation was developed to better understand the model proposed by Strombom et al.
Tools: Matlab

Mobile App Dev

My team and I developed LowCal, the Philippines' first hospital-backed preventive health care application. The app primarily functions as a diet-tracking app using a localized food database. The app was developed in collaboration with Voyager Innovations and Cardinal Santos Medical Center, to whom ownership was later transferred. [Press].
Tools: Android, Eclipse
